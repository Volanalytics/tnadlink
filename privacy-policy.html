1. Information We Collect

Personal Information

We may collect personal information about you in various ways when you use our ad serving platform. "Personal information" is information that is linked or reasonably linkable to an identified or identifiable natural person. It does not include de-identified or aggregate information.

Category Examples Purpose
Identifiers Name, email address, phone number, IP address, device ID, cookie identifiers Account creation, authentication, ad delivery, analytics
Commercial Information Products or services purchased, obtained, or considered Ad targeting, campaign optimization
Internet Activity Browsing history, search history, information regarding interaction with websites, applications, or advertisements Ad targeting, frequency capping, analytics, fraud prevention
Geolocation Data Physical location or movements (typically at city or county level) Tennessee regional targeting, analytics
Demographic Information Age, gender, preferences (derived or provided) Ad targeting, audience analysis

Sensitive Personal Information

We limit our collection of sensitive personal information. When necessary for service provision, we collect such information only with your explicit consent. Sensitive personal information may include:

  • Precise geolocation data (more granular than zip code or city level)
  • Health-related information when relevant to healthcare advertising campaigns

Information from Other Sources

We may receive information about you from other sources, including:

  • Our publisher partners
  • Third-party data providers
  • Public databases
  • Social media platforms (when integration is authorized)

2. How We Use Your Information

We process your personal information for purposes that are compatible with the context in which the information was collected. We limit collection to what is adequate, relevant, and reasonably necessary for the intended purpose.

Primary Purposes

  • Ad Delivery and Personalization: To deliver relevant advertisements to users across our publisher network and personalize ad content based on user interests and behaviors.
  • Performance Measurement: To track the effectiveness of advertising campaigns, including impressions, clicks, conversions, and other engagement metrics.
  • Platform Operations: To operate, maintain, and improve our ad serving platform, including troubleshooting technical issues.
  • Tennessee-Specific Targeting: To enable advertisers to target specific regions, cities, or counties within Tennessee.
  • Account Management: To create and manage advertiser and publisher accounts, process payments, and provide customer service.

Secondary Purposes

  • Analytics and Research: To analyze usage patterns, conduct market research, and develop new products or services.
  • Security and Fraud Prevention: To detect and prevent fraudulent activity, unauthorized access, and other potential security issues.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes.

Note: We will not process your personal information for purposes that are incompatible with those disclosed here without your consent or as otherwise permitted by law.

With Your Consent

We may disclose your personal information for any other purpose with your consent.

4. Tracking Technologies

We and our third-party service providers may use cookies, web beacons, and other tracking technologies to collect and store information about your interactions with our services.

Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use various types of cookies and similar technologies:

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for the operation of our platform.
  • Analytical/Performance Cookies: Allow us to recognize and count visitors and analyze how users navigate our platform.
  • Functionality Cookies: Used to recognize you when you return to our platform and to personalize content.
  • Targeting Cookies: Record your visits to our platform, the pages you visit, and the links you follow to deliver relevant advertisements.

How We Use Tracking Technologies

  • Deliver and measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns
  • Analyze user behavior and preferences
  • Limit the number of times a user sees a particular advertisement (frequency capping)
  • Remember user preferences and settings
  • Detect and prevent fraudulent activity

Your Choices Regarding Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. Additionally, we provide mechanisms for you to opt out of targeted advertising. For more information on managing cookies and your choices, please visit our Cookie Policy.

5. Your Privacy Rights

Under the Tennessee Information Protection Act (TIPA), Tennessee residents have certain rights regarding their personal information. These rights include:

Right to Access

You have the right to confirm whether we are processing your personal information and to access your personal information that we have collected.

Right to Correction

You have the right to request that we correct inaccuracies in your personal information, considering the nature of the information and our processing purposes.

Right to Deletion

You have the right to request that we delete your personal information that we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.

Right to Data Portability

You have the right to obtain a copy of your personal information in a portable and readily usable format that allows you to transmit the data to another entity.

Right to Opt Out

You have the right to opt out of:

  • The processing of your personal information for targeted advertising
  • The sale of your personal information
  • Profiling in furtherance of decisions that produce significant effects concerning you

How to Exercise Your Rights

You may exercise your privacy rights by:

  • Submitting a request through our Privacy Portal
  • Emailing us at info@tnadlink.com
  • Calling us at: 615-671-7672

Response Timeline

We will respond to your request within 45 days. If necessary, we may extend this period by an additional 45 days, provided we inform you of the extension within the initial 45-day period.

Appeal Process

If we decline to take action on your request, you have the right to appeal our decision. You may submit an appeal through the same channels listed above. We will respond to your appeal within 60 days.

Note: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your privacy rights under TIPA.

6. Security of Your Information

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to maintain the security of your personal information, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

Our Security Measures

  • Encryption of personal information in transit and at rest
  • Network security protections including firewalls and intrusion detection
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
  • Access controls and authentication procedures
  • Employee training on data security and privacy practices
  • Incident response procedures

However, please understand that no method of electronic storage or transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. We determine the appropriate retention period based on:

  • The amount, nature, and sensitivity of the personal information
  • The potential risk of harm from unauthorized use or disclosure
  • The purposes for which we process the information and whether we can achieve those purposes through other means
  • Applicable legal, regulatory, tax, accounting, or other requirements
